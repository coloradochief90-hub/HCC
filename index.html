Trading Handbook
Living playbook — philosophy, strategies, red flags, lessons
The Mantra
"I trade what the market is offering — not what I wish it would give. I read the environment before I risk a dollar. I define my entry, my stop, and my target before I pull the trigger. I honor my stop without exception. I stop trading when I hit my daily loss limit. The environment sets the ceiling. The setup earns the size. My discipline determines the outcome."
Playbook Conclusion — Permanent
The market is always offering something. The edge comes from correctly identifying what it is offering, deploying the right strategy for that environment, and executing with discipline. Every trading space below operates on this same foundation — the rules change, the principle does not.
Low Float Small Cap
Low float stocks with real catalysts, breaking highs, and holding the 9 EMA in the right macro environment produce repeatable explosive moves. Systematic rules-based execution in the right environment compounds into a profitable trading career. When the environment is compromised — adapt, don't force.
Mid Cap Momentum
The same catalyst-driven setup logic applied to larger, cleaner order books. When spoofing or a hostile small cap environment makes low float unworkable, mid cap momentum preserves the skill set while protecting capital. Smaller percentage moves, more reliable execution.
Large Cap Earnings
Known catalyst dates, deep liquidity, zero manipulation. The reaction to the event — not the event itself — is the trade. Preparation is everything when the timing is predictable.
Sector ETF Swing
When the cycle phase is clearly identified, trading the leading sector ETF captures the macro move without individual stock risk. No manipulation, no spoofing, no earnings surprise. The trend is the edge.
Macro Theme Trades
The dominant narrative provides the tailwind. Technicals provide the entry and exit discipline. Multi-week positions in the right names at the right time in the cycle — patience is the edge.
Volatility Products
In extreme fear environments volatility itself becomes the trade. Short-term only — decay is the enemy of patience here. Study before capital is committed. The edge is knowing exactly when to enter and when to exit before the fear subsides.
This conclusion is permanent. Every rule in every playbook exists to protect it.
Universal Risk Rules — Apply to Every Trade in Every Space
Define the stop before you enter — never after
Stop placed at chart-defined support — never a P&L number
Move stop to break even once the trade is working
Honor the stop without exception — no averaging down into losers
Day is done when the daily loss limit is hit — no exceptions, no revenge
Pre-determine the target before entry based on chart resistance
Set the target as a limit order — remove the exit decision from emotion
Market Attention Principle — Universal
Catalyst = legitimacy filter only. Not a ranking system.
Price action + volume = priority ranking — always
A failing catalyst name ranks below a no-news name with superior chart structure
Market attention IS a valid catalyst in any space
Release pre-session bias. Flow with what IS happening — not what you expected.
Trade Frequency Framework — Universal
Trade count is driven by qualifying opportunity — not by market condition or a daily target. The risk controls in poor environments are position size and profit target — not an artificial cap on execution. If five setups that qualify appear, take five. If none appear, take zero. The market is the governor, not a rule.
What ends the session naturally — in any space:
Daily loss limit reached
No qualifying setups remain in the window
Attention fading — execution quality declining in real time
Environment First — Universal
The environment is the dominant external force. You don't choose it — you read it. It sets the outer boundary of what's possible that day. No matter how good the setup, you cannot manufacture edge in a market that isn't giving it.
Read the environment before assessing any setup
Environment determines the ceiling — setup rank earns within it
When your primary space is compromised — rotate. Don't force.
Cash is always a valid position. Zero risk has real value.
Behavioral Discipline — Universal
Stop-and-Reset after every losing trade — close the chart, 3 questions, re-evaluate independently
Anchor bias: 2nd entry on same failing setup — evaluate independently. 3rd entry — stand down.
Cannot explain the setup in 2 sentences — it does not qualify regardless of space
Chasing a move that is already extended past its explosive phase — pass it
P&L state must not drive exit decisions — the chart is the exit signal, not the number
Disrupted sleep = mandatory modified protocol in any trading space
Primary Trading Space
Float under 20M. Catalyst or momentum driven. High volatility intraday moves. First to die in risk-off, first to explode in risk-on. All rules below are specific to this space.
Expansion Space — Building
$2B–$10B market cap. Same catalyst-driven setup logic. More liquidity, less spoofing. Viable when small cap order books are compromised. Framework builds as live data accumulates.
Characteristics
Market cap $2B–$10B — institutional participation, still significant intraday moves
More liquid — tighter spreads, cleaner order books, no spoofing concern
Catalyst-driven setups apply — same entry logic as low float thesis trades
Smaller percentage moves — adjust share size to maintain dollar exposure
Less retail-driven — momentum fades slower, holds longer than low float
Most natural first extension — start here when small cap space is compromised

Expansion Space — Building
Apple, NVIDIA, Google, Meta class. Earnings catalyst plays on known dates. No spoofing. Tight spreads. Known catalyst timing allows full preparation.
Characteristics
Primary use case: earnings plays on known dates — Apple, NVIDIA, Google, Meta
Pattern: the reaction to the event — not the event itself
Known date and time — full preparation possible, no surprise timing
High liquidity, tight spreads, zero spoofing concern
Technically similar to catalyst approach — different scale

Tier 1 — Always
Primary
Thesis Trade
A pre-session plan built at 7 AM when your brain is calm. A specific stock, a specific level, a specific trigger, a pre-set target, and a chart-defined stop. The decision was made before the open — not during it.
Built during morning prep — before the open
Specific entry level written down, not approximated
Confirmation signal required (9×20 cross, MACD flip, volume)
Target set as limit order before pulling trigger
Stop placed below chart support — never a P&L number
PRIMARY ticker declared before open — no switching mid-session
When two tickers compete at the open: the PRIMARY (declared in morning prep) is always the thesis trade. If primary isn't firing, only then drop into momentum scalp on the secondary. This decision is made at 7 AM — not at 9:32 when adrenaline and two moving charts compete for attention.
Tier 2 — When Thesis Absent or Wrong
Secondary
Momentum / Market Attention Scalp
No-news, market-attention driven setups. Float + volume + price action = the entire thesis. Fast entries, defined targets, no overstaying. The release valve that keeps you in the game when the plan doesn't materialize.
Gold Standard Profile
Float: Under 5M shares — true low float with explosive potential
Pre-market volume: 500K+ shares with clear price action
Market attention confirmed — showing on scanner, social volume
Price holding above VWAP with 9 EMA rising
MACD 5-min positive — not rolling over at entry
No competing dominant catalyst names on scanner (Low Headline Day)
Activated only when thesis is absent or has been proven wrong
Smaller position size than thesis trades
Faster exit targets — next visible resistance only
Still requires 9 EMA, MACD, VWAP confirmation
5% stop — hard — no adding to losers
Adding to a losing scalp = revenge trading. Exit immediately.
Trade Type 03 — Structure Based
Building Data
Channel Trading
Ascending and descending support/resistance channel setups. Price action contained within defined trend lines. Structure-based entries with clear geometric invalidation points.
Ascending Channel — Entry Criteria
Minimum 2 confirmed higher lows establishing ascending support
Price pulling back to touch ascending support line
Volume drying up on the pullback — sellers exhausted
9 EMA rising and acting as dynamic support
MACD 5-min positive — confirms macro trend intact
Entry near the ascending support touch point
Exit Criteria
Primary target: ascending resistance line (upper channel)
Pre-mark target before entry — set limit order
Stop: 5% below entry OR break of ascending support — whichever is tighter
Move stop to break even when halfway to target
Exit early signal: volume exhaustion at resistance + long wicks
Hard exit: ascending support line breaks with volume
Gold Standard — UGRO Mar 25
200 EMA at $8.63 as channel floor anchor — dual confirmation
Minimum 2 higher lows. 3+ = high confidence
Prior catalyst validates the trend — channel is continuation phase
Both MACD timeframes positive, 5-min not rolling over
Descending Channel: Framework only — insufficient data. Will be populated as patterns are identified in live sessions.
Abort Conditions — All Trade Types
MACD LINE crossed below signal line on 5-min (histogram gone negative) — NOTE: histogram compression during 9 EMA pullback is normal, not a rollover. Check line position vs signal.
Ask side heavily stacked near HOD not absorbing
Volume surging on red candles — distribution

📰 The Low Headline Day Principle
Few or no new catalysts in the low float small cap universe → trader attention concentrates on previous day movers → continuation plays run cleaner and hold longer.
Multiple strong small cap catalysts hitting the same morning → attention fragments across names → each one front-loads faster and fades sooner.
⚠️ Important
This principle operates entirely within the low float small cap momentum space. It describes how momentum trader attention is distributed across scanner names on a given morning — not whether the macro tape is quiet or noisy. Macro headlines are a separate variable.
How to Use in Morning Prep
Low small cap headline day: Higher confidence in continuation setups on prior day movers. Wider targets. More patience after entry. Let winners run to next resistance.
High small cap headline day (2–3+ fresh catalyst names): Attention is split. Each name front-loads faster. Tighter targets, faster exits. Prioritize the single dominant name — most pre-market volume and clearest structure. Don't spread across multiple names.
Assess the scanner — not the news ticker at 7:00 AM MST. How many names with real catalysts are showing up? That number determines the day's attention dynamics.
Observed live: CYCN Apr 1 — dominated a fragmented tape because the merger catalyst was uniquely strong. ARTL Mar 27 — continued cleanly on a low small cap catalyst day. Added to playbook March 2026, clarified April 2026.

App Instructions
How to use the Hendry Command Center
Daily Flow

1. Open HCC — lands on Daily Briefing. Review family schedule and quick-nav to any family member.

2. Go to David → Command Center — review personal streaks, trading snapshot, and failure tracker. Hard gate at the bottom must be passed before accessing any David sub-domain.

3. Pass the gate — "I'm Ready — Enter" clears once per day via localStorage. Resets automatically at midnight.

4. Trade — access Trade Prep for pre-session checklist, market condition, loss limit, and quick links to Handbook and Data Log.

5. Log trades — David → Daily Log → Trading section. One trade at a time. Submit clears form for next trade.

6. Log diet and fitness — Daily Log → Diet and Fitness sections. Diet meters update live as you type. Fitness toggles track goal completion.

7. Evening — check HCC Completion for any pending build tasks. Review domain focus cards.

Navigation

Sidebar — tap the ☰ menu button on mobile. On desktop the sidebar is always visible. Domain names with › expand to show sub-sections.

Accent colors — every domain has its own neon accent color. The topbar title and page headers shift to that color when you navigate into a domain.

Claude + Gemini buttons — bottom of sidebar. Tap to open either tool directly.

Back to briefing — tap Daily Briefing in the sidebar at any time.

Daily Log — How It Works

Trading — fill in all fields for one trade. Tap Submit — form clears and shows confirmation. Repeat for each trade. Data will push to Trading Performance Log Google Sheet when integration is live.

Diet — enter macros and the meters appear automatically showing actual vs target. Tap Log Diet when done.

Fitness — tap toggle buttons for Exercise, Iso Holds, Stretch — they turn neon blue when on. Select workout type, enter steps, flights, intensity minutes, and body battery. Tap Log Fitness.

Updating the App

HCC is hosted on GitHub Pages. When Claude builds an updated version:

1. Download the new HTML file from Claude

2. Save as index.html in your local GitHub Desktop folder (replaces old file)

3. Open GitHub Desktop → Commit → Push Origin

4. Wait 60-90 seconds — both iPhone and desktop PWA update automatically

Error Recovery
Mistakes, undo, and data protection
Trade Log Errors

Before submit: Review all fields before tapping Submit. Once submitted, data writes to Google Sheets.

After submit — minor error: Open the Trading Performance Log in Google Sheets directly and edit the cell. Google Sheets saves instantly.

After submit — major error: Google Sheets keeps full version history. File → Version History → See Version History. Roll back to any previous version.

Soft delete (coming Phase 2C): Rows will be marked as voided rather than deleted — data stays but doesn't count in calculations.

Calendar & Task Errors

Deleted calendar event: Goes to Google Calendar trash. Open Google Calendar → Other Calendars → Trash → Restore. Available for 30 days.

Deleted task: Google Tasks doesn't have a trash — deletion is permanent. HCC will show a confirm dialog before deleting any task.

Wrong event edit: Google Calendar keeps edit history — right-click event → See edit history to restore previous version.

App Data (localStorage)

Gate state: Stored in browser localStorage with today's date as key. Resets automatically at midnight. If gate doesn't reset — clear browser cache or use a different browser session.

Checklist state: HCC Completion checkbox states stored in localStorage. Persist between sessions. To reset — clear site data in browser settings.

Form data: Daily Log forms do not save drafts — if you navigate away before submitting, data is lost. Complete and submit each section before navigating.

Project Life Connect
Commercial life OS — bridges Apple, Google, Microsoft, and everything else
The One-Line Pitch
"One app that reads and writes across everything you already use — Google, Apple, Microsoft, Garmin, and more — organized around your actual life."
The Core Problem
Most people's digital lives are fragmented across 3-5 ecosystems that don't communicate. iPhone with Apple Health, Google Calendar shared with family, Gmail, OneDrive from work, Garmin watch, banking apps. None of it talks to each other. Every app is an island. There is no single place to see and manage your full life.
The Solution
Life Connect sits on top of what users already have. It doesn't replace Google Calendar or Apple Health — it connects them all into one intelligent interface. The app reads and writes across every platform simultaneously. Zero migration. Zero data loss. The user's data stays in systems they already trust and pay for.
Key Differentiators
→Existing infrastructure as the backend. User's own Google Drive, iCloud, or OneDrive stores their data. No data liability. No per-user storage cost. Instant trust.
→Bidirectional sync. Not just a dashboard — reads AND writes back to source systems. Add to Google Calendar, check off tasks, log health data — all from one place.
→Intelligent onboarding. Q&A setup that detects what the user already uses and configures the app around their existing ecosystem automatically.
→Life-domain organization. Not organized by app — organized by life. Family. Health. Work. Finances. Trading. Kids. Personal growth. Each domain pulls from whatever tools feed it.
→AI layer built in. Review, coaching, analysis, and insights powered by AI — accessible within the same system where the data lives.
Target Market
→Families — shared calendar, kids development tracking, household management, spouse coordination
→Professionals — cross-platform work/life integration, performance tracking, goal management
→Health-focused users — fitness, nutrition, sleep, readiness scoring across Garmin, Apple Health, Google Fit
→Mixed-ecosystem users — the majority who use partial Apple + partial Google + partial Microsoft and have no unified view
Pricing Tiers
Trial — 30 days free, full access, no credit card required
Light — Core integrations, 3 domains, basic logging. ~$4.99/month
Pro — All integrations, unlimited domains, AI layer, family sharing, advanced analytics. ~$12.99/month

Why Existing Infrastructure is the Edge
User data lives in their own Google Drive, iCloud, or OneDrive. Life Connect never stores sensitive personal data on its own servers — it only stores connection tokens and preferences. This eliminates data liability, GDPR complexity per user, and per-user storage costs. It also eliminates the #1 objection to new productivity apps — "I don't want to move all my data." There is nothing to move. The user's existing infrastructure becomes the backend.

Life Connect becomes the universal cockpit for personal and family life. Legal & Protection
Patents, API terms, privacy, and risks
Idea Protection — Current Status
Conversations with Claude are private and not shared with other users. However, conversations are not a form of legal IP protection. The concept itself (unified life OS bridging ecosystems) is not patentable in the abstract — the specific implementation, onboarding methodology, and architecture are what can be protected. The best protection right now is execution speed.
Patent Strategy
When a working prototype exists: file a provisional patent application (~$320 USPTO fee for small entity). This grants 12 months of "patent pending" status. Potentially patentable: the Q&A onboarding system that auto-configures a personal OS based on detected user infrastructure, combined with the bidirectional sync architecture across competing ecosystems. Consult a patent attorney when prototype is ready.
Big Company Relationships
GoogleMost open. APIs well-documented. Actively encourages ecosystem building. Low risk.
MicrosoftIncreasingly open under Nadella. Cross-platform strategy aligns with concept. Low-medium risk.
AppleMost restrictive. History of rejecting apps that replicate core iOS features. HealthKit requires native code. App Store approval is the highest risk. Design carefully around their guidelines.
Legal Requirements at Launch
→Privacy policy — required by App Store, Google Play, and GDPR/CCPA
→Terms of service — protects the business from user misuse claims
→LLC formation recommended before accepting payments (~$50-500 depending on state)
→Data processing agreements for EU users if marketing internationally
→Stripe terms compliance for payment processing
Key Risk: Existing Infrastructure as Backend
The biggest risk of the "user's own storage" model is API dependency. If Google changes its API terms, restricts third-party access, or charges for previously free endpoints, it directly impacts the product. Mitigation: build on the most stable, well-documented APIs. Diversify so no single platform is a single point of failure. Keep the app functional in read-only mode even if write access is restricted.

Reference Library
Study material — market cycles, sector rotation, indicators, expansion
Markets move in cycles not straight lines. Every bull run and bear market is part of a larger repeating pattern. Understanding which phase you are in determines which strategies have edge.
Phase 01 — Accumulation
Markets bottom out — choppy sideways price action, low volume
Smart money quietly buying while retail panic sells or stays away
Sentiment bearish or indifferent — rarely looks obvious at the time
Phase 02 — Markup (Bull)
Sustained upward movement — higher highs, higher lows, breadth expanding
Longest and most profitable phase — small caps thrive in risk-on environment
FOMO drives the later stage — watch for distribution signs
Phase 03 — Distribution
Smart money offloading into strength — retail most bullish at the exact top
Erratic price action, new highs then sharp reversals, narrowing breadth
Volume spikes on down days — easy to misread as a pause
Phase 04 — Markdown (Bear)
Lower highs, lower lows — capital fleeing to safety
Bear rallies sharp and short — trap for bulls
Small caps hit hardest and first. Ends at capitulation.
The 6–9 Month Lead Principle
Markets lead the economy by 6–9 months. When recession is declared the bottom may already be forming. When GDP headlines are great distribution may already be underway. Ask: where is the market in its cycle — not where is the economy today?

Universal
Trading Handbook
The Mantra
"I trade what the market is offering — not what I wish it would give. I read the environment before I risk a dollar. I define my entry, my stop, and my target before I pull the trigger. I honor my stop without exception. I stop trading when I hit my daily loss limit. The environment sets the ceiling. The setup earns the size. My discipline determines the outcome."
Playbook Conclusion — Permanent
The market is always offering something. The edge comes from correctly identifying what it is offering, deploying the right strategy for that environment, and executing with discipline. Every trading space below operates on this same foundation — the rules change, the principle does not.
Low Float Small Cap
Low float stocks with real catalysts, breaking highs, and holding the 9 EMA in the right macro environment produce repeatable explosive moves. Systematic rules-based execution in the right environment compounds into a profitable trading career. When the environment is compromised — adapt, don't force.
Mid Cap Momentum
The same catalyst-driven setup logic applied to larger, cleaner order books. When spoofing or a hostile small cap environment makes low float unworkable, mid cap momentum preserves the skill set while protecting capital. Smaller percentage moves, more reliable execution.
Large Cap Earnings
Known catalyst dates, deep liquidity, zero manipulation. The reaction to the event — not the event itself — is the trade. Preparation is everything when the timing is predictable.
Sector ETF Swing
When the cycle phase is clearly identified, trading the leading sector ETF captures the macro move without individual stock risk. No manipulation, no spoofing, no earnings surprise. The trend is the edge.
Macro Theme Trades
The dominant narrative provides the tailwind. Technicals provide the entry and exit discipline. Multi-week positions in the right names at the right time in the cycle — patience is the edge.
Volatility Products
In extreme fear environments volatility itself becomes the trade. Short-term only — decay is the enemy of patience here. Study before capital is committed. The edge is knowing exactly when to enter and when to exit before the fear subsides.
This conclusion is permanent. Every rule in every playbook exists to protect it.
Universal Risk Rules — Apply to Every Trade in Every Space
Define the stop before you enter — never after
Stop placed at chart-defined support — never a P&L number
Move stop to break even once the trade is working
Honor the stop without exception — no averaging down into losers
Day is done when the daily loss limit is hit — no exceptions, no revenge
Pre-determine the target before entry based on chart resistance
Set the target as a limit order — remove the exit decision from emotion
Market Attention Principle — Universal
Catalyst = legitimacy filter only. Not a ranking system.
Price action + volume = priority ranking — always
A failing catalyst name ranks below a no-news name with superior chart structure
Market attention IS a valid catalyst in any space
Release pre-session bias. Flow with what IS happening — not what you expected.
Trade Frequency Framework — Universal
Trade count is driven by qualifying opportunity — not by market condition or a daily target. The risk controls in poor environments are position size and profit target — not an artificial cap on execution. If five setups that qualify appear, take five. If none appear, take zero. The market is the governor, not a rule.
What ends the session naturally — in any space:
Daily loss limit reached
No qualifying setups remain in the window
Attention fading — execution quality declining in real time
Environment First — Universal
The environment is the dominant external force. You don't choose it — you read it. It sets the outer boundary of what's possible that day. No matter how good the setup, you cannot manufacture edge in a market that isn't giving it.
Read the environment before assessing any setup
Environment determines the ceiling — setup rank earns within it
When your primary space is compromised — rotate. Don't force.
Cash is always a valid position. Zero risk has real value.
Behavioral Discipline — Universal
Stop-and-Reset after every losing trade — close the chart, 3 questions, re-evaluate independently
Anchor bias: 2nd entry on same failing setup — evaluate independently. 3rd entry — stand down.
Cannot explain the setup in 2 sentences — it does not qualify regardless of space
Chasing a move that is already extended past its explosive phase — pass it
P&L state must not drive exit decisions — the chart is the exit signal, not the number
Disrupted sleep = mandatory modified protocol in any trading space
